# 02 Evaluate Business

## Purpose

Evaluate business quality after the business model has been understood.

This step evaluates the business itself, not the stock. Do not perform valuation, estimate intrinsic value, or make an investment recommendation.

## Required Inputs

Use the completed outputs from:

- `.work/key_variables.md`
- `01_understand_business.md`

If the business model is incomplete or unclear, state what is missing and how that limits the business quality evaluation.

## Required Sections

Use these sections exactly.

## 1. Business Quality

Assess whether the business appears capable of creating economic value over long periods.

Use the key variables to focus the evaluation. Assess whether each key variable strengthens, weakens, or complicates the business quality judgment.

Focus on the most important drivers, such as:

- Customer value proposition.
- Demand durability.
- Pricing power.
- Unit economics.
- Operating leverage.
- Capital intensity.
- Reinvestment runway.
- Industry position.

Separate directly supported facts from interpretations. Avoid broad claims that are not tied to evidence.

## 2. Moat and Durability

Assess what protects the business economics from competition and what could weaken those protections.

Consider only evidence-supported sources of durability, such as:

- Switching costs.
- Network effects.
- Scale advantages.
- Brand strength.
- Cost advantages.
- Regulatory or licensing barriers.
- Distribution advantages.
- Customer relationships.

Also identify threats to durability, including competition, technology change, regulation, customer concentration, supplier power, substitutes, and changes in industry structure.

## 3. Management and Capital Allocation

Assess management behavior, incentives, capital allocation decisions, and evidence of rational decision making.

Focus on:

- Capital allocation history.
- Reinvestment decisions.
- Mergers, acquisitions, and divestitures.
- Share repurchases and issuance.
- Dividends.
- Leverage decisions.
- Insider ownership and incentives.
- Communication quality and consistency.

Do not assume management quality from stock performance. Use evidence from filings, letters, transcripts, proxy statements, and historical decisions.

## 4. Financial Quality

Assess whether the financial statements support the business narrative.

Focus on:

- Profitability.
- Cash generation.
- Reinvestment.
- Balance sheet strength.
- Capital efficiency.

Check whether reported earnings, cash flow, margins, returns on capital, working capital, debt, dilution, and capital expenditures are consistent with the described business model.

State any accounting issues, adjustments, one-time items, or disclosure gaps that affect confidence.

## 5. Synthesis

Identify the most important findings.

Must include:

- Strongest aspect of the business.
- Weakest aspect of the business.
- Most important uncertainty.
- Overall business quality assessment.

The synthesis should be concise and should not become a valuation or recommendation.

After completing the synthesis, update `.work/key_variables.md` only if the business evaluation changes which variables matter, the current view, the evidence, or what would change the view.

## Rules

- Do not perform valuation.
- Do not estimate intrinsic value.
- Do not make investment recommendations.
- Distinguish facts from interpretations.
- Explicitly identify uncertainty.
- Prioritize the most important findings rather than generating long checklists.
- Do not treat all factors as equally important.
- Do not use market price, recent stock performance, or popularity as evidence of business quality.

## Completion Check

This step is complete when:

- All five required sections are completed.
- `.work/key_variables.md` has been updated if the business evaluation changed the key variables.
- The strongest and weakest aspects of the business are identified.
- Facts and interpretations are distinguishable.
- Material uncertainties are stated.
- No valuation, intrinsic value estimate, or investment recommendation has been included.
