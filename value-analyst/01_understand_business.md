# 01 Understand Business

## Purpose

Build a working model of how the business functions before any evaluation occurs.

The goal is understanding, not judgment. Do not assess business quality, moat, management, valuation, or investment attractiveness in this step.

## Required Inputs

Use the completed source log from `00_required_sources.md` before beginning this step.

If required sources are missing, state what is missing and how that limits the business understanding.

## Required Questions

Answer each question using evidence from reviewed sources:

1. Who pays the company?
2. Why do they pay?
3. What keeps them paying?
4. What could cause them to stop paying?
5. What are the 1-3 most important drivers of long-term business outcomes?

## Required Business Model Description

Describe the following without evaluation:

- Revenue engine: products, services, segments, customers, pricing model, volume drivers, and mix.
- Cost structure: major cost categories, fixed versus variable costs where identifiable, and margin drivers.
- Capital requirements: working capital needs, capital expenditures, reinvestment needs, and asset intensity.
- Industry structure: major participants, value chain position, suppliers, customers, distribution channels, and substitutes.
- Cyclicality: demand sensitivity, replacement cycles, commodity exposure, interest rate exposure, or macro sensitivity where relevant.

## Key Variables Artifact

After answering the required questions, create `.work/key_variables.md`.

This is not a new workflow stage. It is a small living artifact that carries the 1-3 variables most likely to drive long-term outcomes through the existing workflow.

Use this format:

| Key Variable | Current View | Evidence | What Would Change It |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  |  |  |  |

Rules:

- Include only 1-3 variables.
- List variables in decision-importance order. The first row should be the variable most likely to change the final investment judgment. If no single variable dominates, state that uncertainty in the first row rather than forcing one.
- Focus on business drivers, not valuation conclusions.
- Keep each row concise.
- Mark uncertainty rather than forcing confidence.

## Output Format

Use these sections exactly.

### Facts

List directly supported facts from primary sources. Include enough source context that the fact can be checked.

### Interpretations

Explain what the facts imply about how the business works. Keep interpretations descriptive, not evaluative.

Allowed:

- "Revenue appears mainly driven by transaction volume and take rate."
- "The business model appears more asset-light than manufacturing peers based on disclosed capex and cost categories."

Not allowed:

- "The company has a strong moat."
- "Management is excellent."
- "The stock is cheap."
- "This is a buy."

### Open Questions

List unresolved questions, missing disclosures, unclear mechanics, or source limitations.

For each open question, state why it matters for understanding the business.

## Rules

- Do not evaluate moat.
- Do not evaluate management.
- Do not evaluate valuation.
- Do not make investment recommendations.
- Do not introduce company-specific opinions unsupported by reviewed sources.
- Separate facts from interpretations.
- Mark uncertainty explicitly.
- Stop if the business model cannot be described from available sources.

## Completion Criteria

This step is complete only when:

- All five required questions are answered or explicitly marked unresolved.
- `.work/key_variables.md` has been created with 1-3 key variables.
- Revenue engine, cost structure, capital requirements, industry structure, and cyclicality have been described or explicitly marked unavailable.
- The output contains `Facts`, `Interpretations`, and `Open Questions`.
- No moat, management, valuation, or recommendation analysis has been included.
