# 04 Challenge Thesis

## Purpose

Challenge the current investment thesis as an error test.

The goal is not pessimism, balance, or completeness. The goal is to identify realistic reasons the expectation gap may be wrong.

This step exists to reduce confirmation bias and force explicit consideration of disconfirming evidence, fragile assumptions, and important unknowns.

## Required Inputs

Use the completed outputs from:

- `.work/key_variables.md`
- `01_understand_business.md`
- `02_evaluate_business.md`
- `03_assess_investment.md`

If prior steps are incomplete, state what is missing and how that limits the challenge process.

## Required Sections

Use these sections exactly.

## 1. Current Thesis Summary

Summarize the current investment thesis.

Focus on the central expectation gap: what the price appears to believe, what the analysis believes differently, and why that difference may matter. Do not repeat earlier analysis unless it is necessary to state the thesis clearly.

## 2. Strongest Bear Case

Assume the thesis is wrong.

Identify the most realistic reason it may fail. Prioritize failure modes tied to the key variables rather than long lists of generic risks.

For each major failure mode, state:

- What would go wrong.
- Why it is realistic.
- What evidence would support it.
- How it would affect the thesis.

## 3. Disconfirming Evidence

Identify evidence that does not fit the thesis.

Highlight:

- Contradictions.
- Weaknesses.
- Unresolved tensions.
- Evidence that supports a competing interpretation.

Distinguish evidence from speculation. If no clear disconfirming evidence has been found, say so and state whether the source base is sufficient to make that absence meaningful.

## 4. Critical Assumptions

Identify assumptions that must be true for the thesis to remain valid.

Prioritize assumptions whose failure would materially change the conclusion.

At least the most important key variable and the most important normalized owner earnings assumption should be tested here unless they are no longer thesis-relevant.

For each critical assumption, explain:

- What must be true.
- Why it matters.
- What evidence currently supports it.
- What evidence would weaken it.

## 5. Unknowns

Identify important things that remain unknown.

Distinguish unknowns from known risks.

For each unknown, explain:

- What is unknown.
- Why it matters.
- How it could affect confidence.
- Whether it can be researched further.

## 6. Thesis Resilience

After reviewing the counterarguments, determine whether the thesis appears:

- Strengthened.
- Unchanged.
- Weakened.

Explain why. The explanation should focus on whether the bear case, disconfirming evidence, critical assumptions, and unknowns materially changed the thesis.

After completing the challenge, update `.work/key_variables.md` only if the error test changes which variables matter, the current view of a variable, the evidence supporting it, or what would change the view.

## Required Separation

Explicitly separate the following:

- Known Risks: identifiable risks with some supporting evidence or clear business relevance.
- Critical Assumptions: beliefs that must hold for the thesis to work.
- Unknowns: important information that is not currently known.

Do not merge these categories.

## Rules

- Do not perform additional valuation.
- Do not make a final `BUY`, `WATCH`, `PASS`, or `SELL` recommendation.
- Do not repeat earlier analysis unnecessarily.
- Do not create exhaustive risk inventories.
- Prefer realistic failure modes over extreme scenarios.
- Challenge the thesis honestly, even if it appears attractive.
- Distinguish evidence from speculation.
- State when the available source base is insufficient to challenge the thesis confidently.
- Keep the challenge tied to the expectation gap. Generic risk lists are lower value than one decisive error test.

## Completion Check

This step is complete when the output clearly identifies:

- The strongest realistic reason the expectation gap may be wrong.
- Disconfirming evidence, or a clear statement that none was found.
- Critical assumptions.
- Unknowns.
- Known Risks, Critical Assumptions, and Unknowns as separate categories.
- Whether the thesis is Strengthened, Unchanged, or Weakened.
- No final investment recommendation has been made.
